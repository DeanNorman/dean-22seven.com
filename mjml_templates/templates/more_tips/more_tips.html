Purple money is bucks to the future.
Some spending is good spending, because it increases what you have, or decreases what you owe. For example, putting money into investments or savings, or repaying debt. That’s why the purple spending group is called “Invest-Save-Repay”. The more purple money you spend, the better off you’ll be a year or ten from now.
Budget for the future.
You may want to plan for next month, or even the next year. And you can. At the top of your “Budget” screen, just use the arrow to scroll to the month you want to plan for.
Split transactions.
What if you bought clothing and groceries at the same time? Or drew cash and used some of it for food and some for petrol? You can split these transactions in your 22seven budget. So you know exactly where every rand went.
Create your own categories.
22seven has dozens of categories to choose from, but you may spend money on something that doesn’t fit those. Or you may want to name or identify it in your own way. If so, you can easily create your own categories. add these to 22seven manually.
Change your budget start date.
If you prefer not to think about your money in calendar months, or you don’t budget from the first to last day of each month, you can change your budget start date to whatever suits you.
